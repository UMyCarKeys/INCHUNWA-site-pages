## Our Mission

Inchunwa is a community-rooted Southeastern Tribal tattoo project and podcast aimed at promoting body sovereignty through Indigenous Mississippian identity, cultural tattooing practices, collective learning, and knowledge-sharing across in-person and digital platforms.

## Our Vision

Inchunwa's vision for the future is grounded in education. We strive to be a trusted resource for information on ancestral markings and designs, the historical context of Indigenous tattooing practices, and how these traditions are evolving today. Our educational efforts extend beyond online content and include direct work with communities, collaboration with tattoo practitioners, and relationship-building with cultural knowledge keepers. Through this work, we support the reclamation, protection, and continuation of Southeastern Indigenous tattoo traditions for future generations.

## Our History

Our organization was founded by tribal community members to revitalize a cultural practice that has been asleep and, in some cases, forced underground for over 100 years across many Southeastern Indigenous communities. While the revitalization and stewardship of cultural practices have often been held within tribal governments, this project is led by community members and intentionally invites broader community participation in the work of restoration.

## What Sets Us Apart

Globally, Indigenous tattoo revitalization efforts frequently center tattoo practitioners as the primary drivers and knowledge holders of the movement. Our organization is unique in that we are building a model where community members are not only recipients of ancestral markings, but active participants in sustaining the cultural, relational, and ceremonial foundations of the practice. In this way, community involvement is not peripheral — it is intrinsic to the work of reclaiming ancestral marks.

## Who We Serve

The Inchunwa Project serves Native people — primarily Southeastern Native people — as well as researchers, academics, grant makers, media organizations, and anyone seeking to understand and support the revitalization of Indigenous tattooing traditions.
