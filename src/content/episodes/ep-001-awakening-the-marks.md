In this foundational episode of the Inchunwa Project, we begin at the beginning — what does it mean to awaken a cultural practice that has been forced underground for over 100 years?

We hear from community members who describe their first encounters with the concept of ancestral markings, the complex emotions tied to cultural reclamation, and why this work matters now more than ever.

## What You'll Learn

- The historical context of Southeastern Indigenous tattooing
- How colonization disrupted these traditions
- What "awakening" looks like in practice
- The role of community in cultural reclamation

## Featured Voices

Community members from across Southeastern tribal nations share their personal stories of discovering and connecting with ancestral marking traditions.
