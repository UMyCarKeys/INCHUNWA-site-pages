In this episode from our Community Voices series, we sit down with tattoo practitioners who are actively reviving traditional Southeastern Indigenous tattooing techniques.

These practitioners carry not just skill but responsibility — to the communities they serve, to the ancestors whose knowledge they steward, and to future generations who will inherit this work.

## Topics Covered

- The process of learning traditional techniques
- The relationship between practitioner and community
- How traditional designs are researched and revived
- The difference between cultural tattooing and commercial tattooing

## Why This Matters

The Inchunwa Project is unique in centering community participation alongside practitioner expertise. This episode explores that dynamic in depth.
