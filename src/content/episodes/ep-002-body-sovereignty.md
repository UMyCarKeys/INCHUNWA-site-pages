What does it mean to have sovereignty over your own body? In this episode, we examine the concept of body sovereignty through the lens of Indigenous Mississippian identity.

Ancestral markings are more than art — they are declarations of cultural belonging, statements of self-determination, and living connections to lineage.

## Discussion Points

- Defining body sovereignty in an Indigenous context
- The relationship between body markings and tribal identity
- How colonization severed people from bodily expressions of culture
- Contemporary reclamation as an act of self-determination

## Key Themes

This episode lays the philosophical groundwork for understanding why the Inchunwa Project centers body sovereignty in all of its work.
